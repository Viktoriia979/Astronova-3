Blue Origin – New Glenn

Rocket Development: Blue Origin announced plans to launch its New Glenn rocket again later this year after investigating the cause of its recent test failure. Engineers are making improvements to prepare the heavy-lift rocket for future missions.

James Webb Space Telescope

Star Formation: The James Webb Space Telescope captured incredibly detailed images of the Orion A molecular cloud, one of the closest star-forming regions to Earth. The observations are helping astronomers better understand how stars and planetary systems are born.

(Published: June 26, 2026)

SpaceX

Satellite Launch Record: SpaceX reached a major milestone by launching more satellites into orbit than every other space company and agency in history combined. The achievement highlights the company's rapid expansion of the Starlink satellite network.

(Published: June 26, 2026)

Artemis Program

International Cooperation: Botswana became the 68th country to sign the Artemis Accords, agreeing to support the peaceful and responsible exploration of space alongside NASA and its international partners.

(Published: June 25, 2026)

Lucy Mission

Asteroid Research: NASA's Lucy Mission discovered that the asteroid Dinkinesh is part of a binary system with a tiny peanut-shaped moon called Selam. Scientists also found that Selam wobbles as it rotates, an unusual behavior that may reveal how small asteroid systems form and evolve.

(Published: June 21, 2026)

Falcon 9 Rocket

Commercial Orbit Activities: SpaceX successfully launched a Falcon 9 rocket carrying 24 Starlink satellites into low Earth orbit from Vandenberg Space Force Base. The mission expanded the growing Starlink network, which provides high-speed internet coverage to users around the world.

(Published: June 21, 2026)

Supernova Discoveries

Deep Space Discoveries: NASA's Fermi Gamma-ray Space Telescope uncovered evidence suggesting that two supernova remnants in the Milky Way may have originated from related stellar explosions. The findings provide new insights into how massive stars evolve and die, helping astronomers better understand the history and structure of our galaxy.

(Published: June 20, 2026)

Europa Clipper

Jupiter Research: NASA's Europa Clipper spacecraft continued its journey toward Jupiter after passing important system checks. When it arrives, it will investigate Europa's icy surface and search for signs that the moon could support life beneath its frozen crust.

(Published: June 18, 2026)

Ariane 6 Rocket

European Space Boosters: The European Space Agency announced that the Ariane 6 rocket successfully launched using upgraded booster technology, achieving its highest payload performance to date. The milestone strengthens Europe's independent access to space and improves the rocket's ability to carry larger satellites and scientific missions into orbit.

(Published: June 17, 2026)

Quantum Physics

Quantum Physics in Space: Astronauts aboard the International Space Station activated an upgraded version of NASA's Cold Atom Lab to investigate the behavior of matter at temperatures near absolute zero. The enhanced facility will allow scientists to study extreme quantum phenomena, including ultra-cold atoms and exotic states of matter, in the unique microgravity environment of space.

(Published: June 16, 2026)
